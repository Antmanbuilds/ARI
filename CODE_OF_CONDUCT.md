# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Treating people with respect, including people you disagree with
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Showing empathy toward other contributors

Behavior that is not acceptable:

- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing other people's private information without explicit permission
- Conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces · GitHub issues and pull requests, the repository discussion area if enabled, and any other channel the project maintainers operate. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Reports of unacceptable behavior may be sent to `conduct@agenticrates.org`. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The privacy and safety of the reporter will be respected.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project leadership.

## Enforcement actions

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when deciding on consequences:

1. **Correction** · a private written warning, with clarity around why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning** · a public warning and a defined period during which no interaction with the people involved is allowed.
3. **Temporary ban** · a temporary ban from any interaction with the project.
4. **Permanent ban** · a permanent ban from any interaction with the project.
